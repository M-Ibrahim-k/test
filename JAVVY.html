Project Name

Javvy Coffee Product Page Redesign

Duration

2 Days (Design Sprint)

My Role

Solo UX Designer Research, UX Audit, Wireframing, High-Fidelity Design, CRO Optimization

Project Goal

The aim was to redesign the Javvy Coffee product page to improve its clarity, hierarchy, and conversion rate. The original page lacked persuasive storytelling, a clear call to action, and trust-building elements. I applied Conversion Rate Optimization (CRO) principles to deliver a layout that balances aesthetics with strategy.

Research

I conducted a brief UX audit and competitor analysis of premium DTC coffee brands (like Blue Bottle & Chamberlain Coffee).
Key findings included:

  • Weak visual hierarchy on the original PDP
  • No urgency, trust signals, or strong social proof
  • Limited clarity around product benefits and flavor notes

I also mapped out a basic CRO framework focusing on:

  • First 5-second impression
  • Scroll behavior triggers
  • Value proposition placement

Intended Audience

Targeted at coffee enthusiasts and everyday online shoppers who value:

  • Premium experiences
  • Quick decision-making
  • Beautiful, sensory design

User Testing Results

I conducted rapid usability feedback with 3 peers using Loom walkthroughs. Key responses included:

  • “Now I actually feel like buying it.”
  • “Looks like a premium brand now.”
  • “The flavor notes section is a nice touch.”

I used this feedback to refine the layout, especially around CTA visibility and trust badges.

Conclusion

This redesign taught me how to apply CRO thinking within a UX process. Instead of just focusing on UI aesthetics, I prioritized persuasion and content flow to influence buyer behavior. It was a fast-paced project that pushed my layout, spacing, and storytelling skills — and it’s one of my favorite mini-case studies to date.
